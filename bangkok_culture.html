Explore Thai Culture

Culture & Traditions. Thai culture is built on deep respect for elders, for the royal family, and for traditions passed down through generations. Temples, known as wats, are more than places of worship; they are the spiritual heart of every neighborhood. Family life in Thailand is strong and close knit, with grandparents, parents, and children often living together under one roof. Everywhere you go, you will notice kindness, calmness, and the graceful way Thai people greet each other with a wai, hands pressed together like a prayer.

The Grand Palace. No trip to Bangkok is complete without seeing the Grand Palace, a stunning blend of gold, marble, and Thai artistry. This historic royal complex was once the official residence of Thai kings and still holds deep cultural importance today. Inside the palace grounds stands the Temple of the Emerald Buddha, one of the most sacred places in Thailand. As you walk through its shining halls and elegant courtyards, you will feel the power of centuries of tradition and royal history.
